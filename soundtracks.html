This is a list of (mostly) completed soundtracks for Bungie’s Marathon (1994) and its sequels, plus fan games for Aleph One, the open-source continuation of the Marathon engine. With the exception of Eternal X 1.3’s monster work-in-progress soundtrack, I’ve attempted to keep this entirely to completed projects.

A quick note on file formats: FLAC is for listening outside the game, and MP3 or Ogg Vorbis for use within it (since Aleph One doesn’t play FLAC). ‘Pseudo-FLAC’ means I didn’t have a lossless source, so I made a facsimile. I ex-plained my process in a document included with the Eternal 1.2 OST.

Marathon 1 Remixes

Craig Hardgrove’s site has a massive number of these. Except as noted, the following aren’t on his site:
  • Tobacco: pseudo-FLAC
  • Cannibal Whore Feast: pseudo-FLAC
  • Chibi-usa: pseudo-FLAC
  • Talashar: Bandcamp (streaming, FLAC, basically any other format you want)
  • CKT1138: YouTube, FLAC & Ogg Vorbis
  • Matrix_XV: YouTube, pseudo-FLAC, Ogg Vorbis/MP3
  • My QuickTime 2.5 remixes: YouTube, FLAC, Ogg Vorbis (faithful to the original sound while adding stereo, reverb, and various other flourishes)
  • QuickTime 2.0/2.5 stereo: YouTube, FLAC (QuickTime 2.0 in one channel, QuickTime 2.5 in the other)
  • Eternal X 1.2 OST: pseudo-FLAC. Craig’s site has the Ogg Vorbis files (but if you have Eternal X 1.2 or 1.2.1, you already have these anyway) and a link to the YouTube video.
  • (Work in progress) Eternal X 1.3 OST: FLAC/pseudo-FLAC (depending on track)
  • Will Christian: SoundCloud, FLAC
Several others (i.e., wowbobwow, Storm, & dontask4470) have begun but not yet finished what I sincerely hope will be remixes of the entire OST. Search their names on the Discord and add ‘has: sound’ to bring them up.

Marathon 2 Special Edition OST

Pseudo-FLAC, MP3. See here for solo Lua scripts to use these with vanilla Marathon 2 maps (even the Windows 95 maps), plus explanations of how to do so. The M2SE itself is available here.
