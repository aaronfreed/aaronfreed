Aaron Freed’s Discography

I got sick of linking people directly to the OneDrive folder where I’ve uploaded all my music: it provides almost no information about each release, and its URL is impossible to remember. So here’s a more comprehensive overview with a more memorable URL. Release dates are in ISO format (yyyy-mm-dd, which has the convenient benefit of sorting automatically when entered into a computer file system) when I know the full date. Releases’ names are followed by their length and TT Dynamic Range Meter score (a higher score indicates less dynamic range compression).

You may also be interested in my Marathon soundtracks page.

Core Discography

  • 1997 – Compositions 1993-1997 (65:17, DR13, album): These are juvenilia, but I’m still proud of parts of them. Saying this has a 1997 release date might not be strictly accurate, since I went back and revised parts of the arrangements in 2008, but the vast majority of it dates to the years listed in the title, and all of this material was previously released (albeit among a small circle of friends and family members). The bulk of this is original composition, but a few are arrangements of a few traditional pieces, and one track, ‘The Colour of Night’, was cowritten with a middle/high school friend, Austin Shoemaker.
  • 1997 – Marathon Remixes (QuickTime) (39:35, DR12, remix album): My first remixes of the Marathon soundtrack. These are extremely faithful to the sound and style of the original tracks, adding only reverb, panning, instrument doubling, and occasional other flourishes to tracks.
  • 1997 – Theme from Marathon Chronicles (2:58, DR12, single): Two different arrangements of a track from Compositions 1993-1997 called ‘The Tempest’, used as the theme for my still-unfinished scenario Marathon Chronicles (see YouTube for more info).
  • 2014-11-24 – Demos 2014 (52:30, DR15, demo album): Competent piano demos featuring an original composition (‘Love’), arrangements of an excerpt from Genesis’ ‘Supper’s Ready’ and two different Nobuo Uematsu tracks entitled ‘Terra’ (one from Final Fantasy VI and the other from Final Fantasy IX), and two improvisations. Actually, even the original song and covers contain plenty of improvisation.
  • 2021-01-28 – Cadence of Hyrule Future Sound Remixes (91:50, DR12, remix album): Counting DLC, Cadence of Hyrule has up to six different versions of its songs, all with identical tempi and melodies. These remixes weave 17 of its songs into dense, psychedelic listening experiences that are radically different from their sources.
  • 2021-02-03 – Marathon QuickTime 2.0 & 2.5 stereo remix (41:00, DR12, remix album): Marathon’s soundtrack was originally in mono, and the instrument voices changed slightly between QuickTime 2.0 and 2.5. I put 2.0 in the left channel and 2.5 in the right. The result is better than you’d expect. The stereo phasing helps.
  • 2022-12-05 – Marathon: Pistol Starts (See You Starside First Drafts) (40:37, DR12, remix album): The first Marathon remixes I did in GarageBand. They’re less faithful in sound than my 1997 remixes (I used ton of retro synth voices, especially the Mellotron), but more stylistically faithful than any of my following mixes, which are still based on these. ‘Pistol Start’ refers to a form of challenge run done by hitting command-option-new game (ctrl-shift-new game on Windows or Linux); a player must complete a level with the starting loadout of a pistol and three clips.
  • 2022-12-07 to present – See You Starside: Interim Revisions (155:35, DR12, remix/arrangement anthology): Rather than remove old mixes entirely as I supersede them, I’ve simply moved them to this folder so that people can trace their evolution. Dates are listed in ISO format, followed by 24-hour time (00.00 to 23.59). My lack of anything that could even charitably be described as a sleep schedule may be apparent from these filenames.
  • 2023 (WIP) – See You Starside: The Marathon Soundtrack Reimagined (73:01, DR13, remix/arrangement album): What began as an endeavour to remix Marathon’s soundtrack grew into something more resembling an arranged album, with (as of this count) nearly 33 minutes of new material. These are dense, intricate mixes; retro synths, dynamic range, reverb, arpeggiation, cross-references to other tracks, and entirely new instrument parts can all be found in copious quantities. The influence of ’70s progressive rock songwriting and arranging, ’80s pop production, and ’90s Japanese game composers should be readily apparent to listeners; there’s also occasional influence from genres as disparate as jazz, blues, disco, post-rock, classical, and metal.

Official Releases

I mastered or remastered the soundtracks used in official releases of the following game mods:

  • 2018-12-21 – Eternal X 1.2 OST (1:31:58, DR12) by Dr Craig Hardgrove, Tommy T-Bone, Nicholas Singer, and Eike Steffen. The most acclaimed and popular remixes of Marathon’s soundtrack, three classic mash-ups of Marathon and Halo tracks, and a few bangin’ originals. YouTube video here.
  • 2021-06-19 – Trojan Standalone Edition OST (1:57:05, DR15) by Tom Worth, John Carlo Maffei, Jason Aguiar, Steve Campbell, and Hamish Sanderson. Technically, I remastered these tracks, though they’re mostly not huge changes from the originals besides having slightly more dynamic range. The biggest change is the inclusion of the credits loop, which I also expanded slightly. (I didn’t credit myself for this.) YouTube video here, though it omits the credits loop and ‘Archangael Death’ for the sake of pacing. My biggest contribution to this release by far was to the sounds, all of which I remastered. This took me 20+ hours, and the only acknowledgement I got was a ‘thanks’; my contributions to the game weren’t mentioned anywhere in the credits. I’m still annoyed about this.
  • 2020-05-24 – Marathon Phoenix OST (1:38:42, DR11) by Kevin MacLeod and CryoS. Included with Phoenix as of version 1.4, released 2022-01-19. (Phoenix 1.4.1, released 2023-01-05, is the latest release.) YouTube video here. I also remastered the sounds, which you can get here. This also includes a remastered version of the theme music.
  • 2022-09-19 – dungeons presents hellpak: Vol. 1 – Not Recommended by Doctors (2:03:46, DR12) by tbcr, Matrix_XV, CKT1138, and NEFX. The official soundtrack, including ten tracks not used in the game. Each track has unique artwork generated with Midjourney. I strongly recommend watching the YouTube video to get the full experience.
  • 2023 (WIP) – dungeons presents hellpak: Vol. 1 – Not Recommended by Doctors DLC (54:35, DR11) by Matrix_XV, tbcr, and CKT1138. Technically not a soundtrack, but it’ll be an official release once we finalise it. It contains remixes and demos of Vol. 1 tracks, plus standalone versions of tracks with gapless transitions on the OST.
  • 2023 (WIP) – dungeons presents hellpak: Vol. 2 – An Exercise in Questionable Taste (2:23:51, DR12) by Matrix_XV, tbcr, CKT1138, and Aaron Freed. Will also contain several NEFX tracks, but I don’t currently have them. We have not even begun to consider the song order yet, so these are not tagged with song numbers. Some of these songs will likely be cut from the final release, though some of those will probably be held over for Vol. 3.
  • 2023 (WIP) – Tempus Irae Redux OST (2:34:45, DR11) by Alexander Nakarada, Rafael Krux, Kevin MacLeod, Bryan Teoh, Nine Inch Nails, Brian Boyko, Sean Magee, Rich Wilcox, and Fungus Amongus (forthcoming). Tempus Irae Redux is nearing the end of its development and will likely be released in early to mid-2023.
  • 2023 (WIP) – Eternal X 1.3 OST (6:03:36, DR12) by Aaron Freed, Dr Craig Hardgrove, wowbobwow, Talashar, wowbobwow, CKT1138, Solar-Tron, Dan Storm, Matrix_XV, Tommy T-Bone, Nicholas Singer, Eike Steffen, and Trey J. Anderson. 1.3 is still under construction, though it’ll likely be finished in mid- to late 2023.
